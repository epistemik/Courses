CSI 2131 Assignment Standards

Marks will be deducted for assignments that don't conform to these standards.

Handing in Assignments

All assignments for students in CSI 2131A are to be handed in to the locked box for CSI 2131A on the third floor of MacDonald Hall, to the left of the old Computer Science window.

All assignments for students in CSI 2131B are to be handed in to the locked box for CSI 2131B on the third floor of MacDonald Hall, to the left of the old Computer Science window.

Assignments must be in a large envelope containing the following items:

  • a 3.5-inch DOS/Windows diskette with only source files and MS-DOS executable files for the current assignment. The names of the files will be specified in each assignment. Your file names must be exactly as specified in the assignment. You will absolutely lose marks for wrong file names, missing files or extra files.
  • a paper printout of your source files
  • stapled sheets of paper for handwritten (or typed) answers

The outside of the envelope, the diskette and every single sheet of paper should be labelled clearly with the following information:

  • your student name and your student number
  • the course code (CSI 2131)
  • the section (A or B)
  • the instructor's name (Ken Barker)
  • the tutorial group (to be determined)
  • the TA's name

Assignments that do not conform to these instructions will be assumed to be for the course ELG 4572 and marked accordingly.

Programming Style

You must program in style. Your TA will show you how in a very early tutorial. TAs have been given the power to deduct many, many marks for a poorly written program, even if the program "works" perfectly.
