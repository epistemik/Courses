Comparison of simple paging and simple segmentation w. dyn. partitions
nSimple segmentation suffers from external fragmentation (this comes from dyn. partitions) nPaging only yields internal fragmentation (1/2 page in average per program) nSimple segmentation may requires more complicated hardware for address translation (addition instead of concatenation) nSegmentation is visible to the programmer whereas paging is transparent nSegment protection and sharing is more logical than page protection because normally one wants protection to be based on logical program portions.
nSegmentation allows dynamic linking of segments.
