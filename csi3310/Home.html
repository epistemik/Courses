CSI 3310 Operating System Principles

(2 hours of lecture per week, 2 hours of lab per week, 3 credits)
May 1 - July 17, 2000.

Batch processing systems. Input/output systems. Interacting processes. Multiprogramming systems. Storage management.
Procedure and data sharing. Process and resource control. Deadlock prevention.
Prerequisites: CSI 2121 or CEG 3391.

Texbook (compulsory)

Other References (optional)

  • Operating System Concepts, A. Silberschatz - P. Galvin, Addison-Wesley, 1997, 5th ED.

Objective

    To obtain a good understanding of the principles behind the functional components of an operating system. Moreover, the laboratories aim at giving you some programming experience (with Java) on concurrency and thread synchronization.

Course content

  • Computer system hardware overview.
  • Operating system overview.
  • Process description and control.
  • Threads.
  • Concurrency: mutual exclusion and synchronization.
  • Concurrency: deadlock and starvation.
  • Memory management.
  • Virtual memory.
  • Uniprocessor scheduling.
  • I/O management (depending on the amount of time left).
