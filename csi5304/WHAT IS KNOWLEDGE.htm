WHAT IS KNOWLEDGE?

 

 

“acquaintance with facts, truths, or principles, as from study or investigation”: (Int Webster Ency. Dict)

 What’s the difference between “knowledge” and “information”?

 What’s the difference between “know” and “inform”?

 (Skuce definitions) 

Knowledge is information that can be possessed by a system capable of “knowing”, i.e. one that has some intelligence, e.g. can learn, generalize, recognize patterns, solve problems, etc. However, k can be stored for use by such systems in other systems that do not “know”, but merely store, process, and retreive. These latter systems could be called “Knowledge Management Systems” 

Information consists of signals that can be input to some system and that affects its behaviour.. Information is “rawer” than knowledge. A system capably of thinking or reasoning can convert information into knowledge.

KNOWLEDGE ENGINEERING VS MANAGEMENT

 

KE: Converting k in whatever form into one suitable for machine processing
