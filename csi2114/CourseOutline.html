Course Description: (from the university calendar)
Introduction to abstract data types. Trees, binary search trees, balanced trees. Searching. Sorting. Simple examples of complexity analysis. Graphs, simple graph algorithms: depth-first and breadth-first search, minimum spanning tree, shortest path. Prerequisites: CSI 1101 or CSI 1102

Course Objectives:

1. To develop skills in specifying, implementing, using and analyzing data structures.
2. To introduce specific widely-used data structures and algorithms.
3. To convey the importance of separating specification from implementation.

Resources: Text book: Thomas A. Standish. 1994. Data Structures, Algorithms and Software Principles. Addison-Wesley.
