PROJECT

Before you start actually writing any code,
  • read very carefully this announcement
  • set a realistic goal for yourself
  • if you decide to go for part of the bonus, make sure your partner has the same intentions
  • for most part, you have to work individually and for that you will be graded separately. in fact, it is possible to achieve 100% ( or more) without having a partner
  • I would prefer if you work with a partner and I expect partners to help each other!, but you will not receive any marks for your contribution in your partner's work!
  • when you find a partner, one of you have to do PROJECT A and the other PROJECT B, if you can integrate your work, the bonus will apply to the two parts equally
  • if you do not understand the rules, make sure you clarify them with me, no later excuses
  • if you decided on a partner, and you are sure, register your group with the TA. he will mark all the projects, and will show up in the Friday labs to answer questions.
  • after March 12, absolutely no project group modifications. That is, you may not change partners, regardless what the circumstances are. if your partner has dropped the course, you can still achieve a 100.
  • DO NOT HAND IN A PRINTOUT OF MY CODE! I wrote most of the code that is needed for the project. You only hand in a printout of your work, not the implementation I gave you!!!
  • cheating will be penalized. you may only submit your own work. it will be very obvious for the marker if you have copied or stolen someone's work. if you cheat on this project and yoo were one of those who were caught on the assignments for cheating (whether you actually got 0 or not) you will receive an F for the entire course. Discussions and help amongst partners and groups is encouraged, however taking someone's code and handing it in as your own (regardless whether you changed variable names, indentation and comments) is considered cheating and can be easily discovered and proven. Partners in the same group can "code" together, but you should understand that your contribution in your partner's work is not going to be credited to you.
  • do not panic, if you are not confident, follow my instructions and hints to the letter and start early to make steady progress.

The Project TA is Mr. Yutian Luo (yluo@site.uottawa.ca). His hours in the Cube: FRIDAYS 18:30 -- 20:00
