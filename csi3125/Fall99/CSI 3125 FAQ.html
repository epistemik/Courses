  1. When should I email the prof?

    The prof has office hours listed on the course syllabus. But sometimes the problem can't wait until office hours. And sometimes the prof is too busy during office hours. So if you can't ask your question during office hours, you might have to email the prof. Here are some things you might try before emailing the prof:

    • ask your question in the class
    • ask your question to the TA in the tutorial
    • read the FAQ... maybe the answer is already there
    • read the notes or the textbook... maybe the answer is in there

    If that stuff is not really your cup of tea, then by all means, email the prof.

  2. I emailed the prof but I didn't get an answer.

    If your email was a simple request (to change tutorial groups, to add something to the web page, etc.), I might just act on the request and not reply. Check the web pages frequently to see if I just went ahead and made the requested update.

    You may also have sent an email with an invalid or missing reply-to field.

  3. I heard that the prof has been emailing the class but I never receive anything.

    You may have specified some invalid email account when you signed up for a lab account in the Cube.

  4. When should I email the prof the source code to my assignment programs?

    Never. (Never ever).

  5. When I send the prof an email with an attachment in it, where does the attachment go?

    /dev/null

  7. Which tutorial group am I in?

    In the first week of class you told me which of the tutorials fit into your schedule. I then assigned you to a particular tutorial group. You must attend the tutorial to which you are assigned. Check the tutorial rosters to find out which tutorial group you have been assigned to.

  8. My student number doesn't appear on the class roster.

    If your student number is not on a class roster, you were not registered to take the course at the time the roster was posted. If you're still not registered, go see your undergraduate academic assistant right away! But I won't update the class roster until the faculty sends me an updated list, so it will not reflect recent registrations or section changes.

  9. What mark do I need to get to pass the course?

    If you are a registered student in a program in the Faculty of Engineering (including CEG, CHG, CSI, CVG, ELG, MCG and SEG) you must get at least a D+ to pass this course. If you are a student from another faculty (Administration, Arts, Science, etc.), your minimum passing grade is set by your own faculty.

  10. You said that something was on the web page for the course, but I loaded the web page into Netscape (Internet Explorer) and I didn't see it.

    If you don't see the information you're looking for on the web page, try hitting the "Reload" ("Refresh") button in Netscape (Internet Explorer).

    If you still don't see the information you're looking for, you may need to check your cache. In Netscape, go to the Edit menu, Preferences, Advanced, Cache, and clear the memory cache, clear the disk cache, and make sure the document in cache is being compared to the network at least once per session. In Explorer, go to the View menu, Internet Options, Temporary Internet files, Settings..., and make sure you're checking for newer versions at least every time you start IE.

  11. I keep clicking on a section in the course notes list, but nothing happens.

    Click on a different section instead (or click on "Pick a Section"). Then click on the original section you wanted to go to. You could also hit the "Reload" ("Refresh") button in Netscape (Internet Explorer).

  12. When will the latest batch of course notes be available on the web?

    Not yet.

  13. In the call graph in Figure 4.3 of the textbook, shouldn't B be able to call A?

    Yes, B should be able to call A, that's just a mistake. But Figure 4.3 also makes some other assumptions. One of the assumptions is that procedures can only call procedures declared earlier in the program. Theoretically, the order of procedure declarations should make no difference. So the call graph in Figure 4.3 should have extra arcs from B to A, from A to B, from C to B and from D to B.
